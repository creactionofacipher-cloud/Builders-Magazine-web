# **Brand Guide**

## **Purpose**

This document defines the identity, values, and personality of Builders Magazine.

The purpose of this guide is to ensure that every design decision, piece of content, interface element, and future feature reflects the same brand identity.

Builders Magazine is more than a magazine—it is a platform dedicated to documenting and preserving custom motorcycle culture.

---

# **Brand Mission**

Builders Magazine exists to celebrate the people, motorcycles, craftsmanship, and stories that shape the custom motorcycle community.

The magazine is not focused on trends or commercial success.

Its mission is to preserve culture, inspire builders, and document exceptional projects for future generations.

---

# **Brand Vision**

To become one of the world’s most respected editorial platforms dedicated to custom motorcycles and the people who build them.

Builders Magazine should be recognized not only for beautiful photography, but also for thoughtful storytelling and editorial quality.

---

# **Core Values**

Everything Builders Magazine creates should reflect these values:

- Authenticity
- Craftsmanship
- Creativity
- Independence
- Respect
- Community
- Quality
- Timelessness

These values take priority over trends and short-term popularity.

---

# **Brand Personality**

If Builders Magazine were a person, it would be:

- Experienced
- Curious
- Passionate
- Honest
- Calm
- Confident
- Respectful
- Detail-oriented

The brand never needs to shout to be noticed.

---

# **What Builders Magazine Is**

Builders Magazine is:

- an editorial publication
- a cultural archive
- a source of inspiration
- a showcase for outstanding motorcycles
- a home for builders and enthusiasts
- a place where craftsmanship is valued

---

# **What Builders Magazine Is Not**

Builders Magazine is not:

- a news website
- a clickbait blog
- a social network
- a motorcycle marketplace
- a corporate media outlet
- an advertising catalog

Editorial independence is essential.

---

# **Audience**

The primary audience includes:

- custom motorcycle builders
- motorcycle enthusiasts
- workshop owners
- photographers
- designers
- collectors
- artists
- event organizers
- people interested in craftsmanship and motorcycle culture

The platform speaks to both experienced builders and newcomers with genuine curiosity.

---

# **Tone of Voice**

Communication should always be:

- authentic
- thoughtful
- respectful
- knowledgeable
- confident
- welcoming

Avoid:

- aggressive marketing
- exaggerated claims
- unnecessary technical jargon
- sensationalism

The tone should encourage conversation rather than sell products.

---

# **Visual Identity**

The visual language should communicate:

- quality
- simplicity
- authenticity
- editorial elegance
- premium photography

The design should never overshadow the content.

Photography is the hero.

---

# **Editorial Identity**

Builders Magazine values stories over specifications.

Technical details matter, but they exist to support the human story behind every motorcycle.

The platform celebrates:

- builders
- ideas
- craftsmanship
- perseverance
- creativity

Not only finished motorcycles.

---

# **Builders Cup**

Builders Cup is an extension of the Builders Magazine brand.

It represents:

- community
- craftsmanship
- competition
- inspiration
- recognition

Coverage should emphasize the people and projects rather than only the results.

---

# **Partnerships**

The magazine welcomes partnerships with brands, workshops, and organizations that share its values.

Commercial collaborations must never compromise editorial independence.

Readers should always trust the integrity of published content.

---

# **Community**

Builders Magazine exists because of its community.

The platform should encourage:

- collaboration
- knowledge sharing
- creativity
- respect
- participation

Every contributor deserves recognition.

---

# **International Perspective**

Although Builders Magazine has its own identity and roots, it aims to connect builders and enthusiasts from around the world.

The platform should remain accessible and relevant to an international audience.

---

# **Brand Evolution**

The platform will continue to evolve by adding:

- new stories
- new builders
- new projects
- new events
- new digital experiences

However, its core identity must remain consistent.

Growth should strengthen the brand, not redefine it.

---

# **Brand Principles**

Every decision should support the following principles:

- Content First
- Photography First
- Authenticity Over Trends
- Quality Over Quantity
- Community Over Commercialization
- Timeless Design
- Editorial Independence
- Respect for Craftsmanship

---

# **Guiding Statement**

Builders Magazine is not simply about motorcycles.

It is about the people who imagine, build, ride, and preserve them.

Every page, every story, and every photograph should honor that mission.
