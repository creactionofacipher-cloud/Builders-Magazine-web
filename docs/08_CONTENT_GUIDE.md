# **Content Guide**

## **Purpose**

This document defines the editorial principles for all content published on the Builders Magazine platform.

The website is an extension of the printed magazine and should maintain the same quality, authenticity, and editorial voice.

Every article should contribute to the culture of custom motorcycles rather than simply report information.

---

# **Editorial Mission**

Builders Magazine exists to document and preserve custom motorcycle culture.

The platform celebrates:

- builders
- motorcycles
- craftsmanship
- workshops
- road stories
- events
- people
- creativity

The goal is to inspire rather than entertain.

---

# **Content Principles**

Every publication should follow these principles:

- Authentic
- Honest
- Educational
- Inspiring
- Timeless
- High quality
- Photography-driven
- Story-focused

Avoid producing content that exists only to generate traffic.

---

# **Writing Style**

Articles should read like magazine features rather than blog posts.

The writing should be:

- calm
- confident
- descriptive
- immersive
- respectful

Avoid:

- clickbait
- sensationalism
- exaggerated marketing language
- unnecessary hype

---

# **Tone of Voice**

Builders Magazine speaks as an experienced member of the custom motorcycle community.

The tone is:

- knowledgeable
- approachable
- passionate
- authentic

Never arrogant.

Never overly technical without explanation.

Never overly promotional.

---

# **Photography**

Photography is an essential part of every publication.

Images should:

- support the story
- reveal craftsmanship
- document details
- create atmosphere

Avoid generic stock photography.

Whenever possible, use original photography.

---

# **Story Categories**

The platform publishes content in the following categories.

---

## **Bike**

Focus:

Motorcycle projects.

Typical structure:

- Introduction
- Background
- Build process
- Technical details
- Riding experience
- Gallery

---

## **Builder**

Focus:

People behind the motorcycles.

Topics:

- biography
- philosophy
- workshop
- inspiration
- projects

---

## **Culture**

Focus:

The custom motorcycle lifestyle.

Examples:

- travel
- history
- craftsmanship
- events
- workshops
- community

---

## **Interview**

Focus:

Conversations with builders, photographers, artists, organizers, and other contributors.

The goal is to reveal personality, experience, and ideas rather than simply collect answers.

---

## **Event**

Focus:

Coverage of motorcycle events.

Articles should capture:

- atmosphere
- participants
- projects
- people
- memorable moments

Not just event schedules or results.

---

# **Article Structure**

A typical article should include:

- Cover image
- Title
- Short introduction
- Main content
- Pull quotes (optional)
- Gallery
- Related stories
- Related bike or builder

Long articles should be divided into meaningful sections.

---

# **Headlines**

Headlines should be:

- concise
- memorable
- descriptive

Avoid:

- clickbait
- excessive punctuation
- ALL CAPS
- vague titles

---

# **Image Guidelines**

Images should:

- be high resolution
- be properly cropped
- have descriptive alt text
- include captions when useful

The first image should always be strong enough to represent the entire story.

---

# **SEO Principles**

Content should be written for people first.

SEO should support discoverability without reducing editorial quality.

Requirements:

- descriptive titles
- meaningful headings
- readable URLs
- natural keyword usage
- descriptive image alt text

Avoid keyword stuffing.

---

# **Multilingual Content**

The platform supports:

- Russian
- English

Translations should preserve tone and meaning rather than follow literal wording.

---

# **Future Content Types**

The editorial model should support future formats, including:

- long-form features
- photo essays
- documentaries
- video stories
- workshop guides
- technical articles
- travel journals
- opinion pieces

---

# **Editorial Standards**

Every published article should meet the following criteria:

- Original content
- Accurate information
- Clear structure
- High-quality photography
- Proper grammar
- Editorial review before publication

Quality should always take priority over publishing frequency.

---

# **Guiding Principle**

Every article should answer one question:

**“Does this story contribute something meaningful to custom motorcycle culture?”**

If the answer is no, it should not be published.
